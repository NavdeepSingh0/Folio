# Clocks and Time Calculations

## Introduction
A clock is a device that measures time, typically displaying hours, minutes, and seconds. Understanding clocks and their mechanics is essential for various applications in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM).

## Key Concepts

### Angle Traversed by Minute Hand and Hour Hand
The minute hand traverses 360° in 60 minutes, while the hour hand traverses 30° in 60 minutes. This results in the minute hand gaining 5° over the hour hand per minute.

### Angle Between Minute and Hour Hand at a Given Time
To find the angle between the minute and hour hands at a given time, we use the formula: `Angle = |30H - (11/2)M|`, where H is the hour and M is the minutes.

## Formulas

* `Angle = 30H - (11/2)M`
* One minute division = 6° apart
* One hour division = 6° × 5 = 30° apart

### Fast and Slow Clocks
A fast clock is one that runs faster than the standard clock, while a slow clock is one that runs slower. In this context, we're interested in calculating time based on a standard clock.

## Mirror Image of the Clock
The mirror image of a clock is obtained by reflecting the hour and minute hands across the vertical axis. This means swapping the hour hand with the minute hand.
