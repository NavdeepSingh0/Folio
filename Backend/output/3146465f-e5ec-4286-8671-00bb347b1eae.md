# CLOCKS: Understanding the Basics and Formulas
## Introduction
A clock is a device with two hands, an hour hand (H.H.) and a minute hand (M.H.), used to measure time. The main concepts of clocks include the angle traversed by both hands and the number of times they coincide in a full or half day.

## Definitions

* **Hour Hand (H.H.)**: The short hand that points to the hour on the clock.
* **Minute Hand (M.H.)**: The long hand that points to the minute on the clock.
* **Second Hand**: The thin hand that moves rapidly around the clock face, used to measure seconds.

## Key Concepts

* One minute division = 6° apart
* One hour division = 30° apart
* In one minute, the M.H. gains 5° over the H.H.
* In one hour, the M.H. gains 330° over the H.H.

## Formulas

* **Angle made by Hands**: 30H − 11/2M

		- M = Minute

## Key Takeaways

* The minute hand moves 6° in one minute and the hour hand moves 30° in one hour.
* In one hour, the minute hand gains 55 minutes divisions over the hour hand.
* The angle made by the hands can be calculated using the formula: 30H − 11/2M.

# Clocks and Angles
## Introduction

A clock is a circular device that displays time, with two hands (hour hand and minute hand) that move to indicate the current hour and minutes. Understanding the angles formed by these hands is crucial in various applications.

## Definitions

* Hour Hand: The short hand that points to the hour on the clock.
* Minute Hand: The long hand that points to the minutes on the clock.
* Angle: The measure of rotation between two points or lines, typically expressed in degrees.

## Key Concepts

### Degrees Moved by Hands

The degree moved by each hand is directly proportional to the time elapsed.

* Hour Hand: 30 degrees per hour, 0.5 degrees per minute
* Minute Hand: 6 degrees per minute

### Angle Between Hands at Specific Times

#### 3 PM:
At 3 PM, the angle between the hour and minute hands is π/4 radians or 45 degrees.

#### 10 Minutes Past 5:
By 10 minutes past 5, the hour hand has turned through 165 degrees (from 12 to 4:30) plus 3.75 degrees for each minute past 5.

#### 3:25 PM:
At 3:25 PM, the angle between the hour and minute hands is approximately 67.5 degrees.

#### 15 Minutes Past 5:
At 15 minutes past 5, the angle between the hour and minute hands is 67.5 degrees.

### Angle Between Hour Hand and Minute Hand

The angle between the hour hand and minute hand can be calculated using the following formula:

tan(θ) = (Minute Hand Movement / 60) * (2π - π/12)

where θ is the angle between the two hands.

### Reflex Angles and Right Angles
A reflex angle is greater than 180 degrees, while a right angle is exactly 90 degrees.

#### Reflex Angle at 10:25
The reflex angle between the hands of a clock at 10:25 is approximately 187.5 degrees (π - θ), where θ is the acute angle between the two hands.

### Occurrences of Right Angles and Opposite Directions

* The hands of a clock are in opposite directions exactly once per hour, resulting in 44 occurrences in a day.
* The hands of a clock are at right angles 22 times in a day.

## Conclusion
Understanding the movements and angles formed by the hour hand and minute hand is crucial for solving various problems related to clocks. By applying mathematical concepts such as trigonometry and geometry, we can calculate the positions and angles of these hands at different times.

### Problem 18: Time of Hand Meeting

The hands of a clock meet at the same time when the angle between them is 180°. Let's consider the angles formed by the minute hand (M) and the hour hand (H):

*   Angle of H from the 12 o'clock position = $30x^\circ$, where x is the hour.
*   Angle of M from the 12 o'clock position = $6y^\circ$, where y is the minutes.

The sum of these angles should be equal to 180°:

$$30x + 6y = 180$$

We want to find when the hands meet between 2 and 3 o'clock. In this case, x = 2 (hour) and we need to solve for y (minutes).

Solving for y:

$$(30 \times 2) + 6y = 180\\
60 + 6y = 180\\
6y = 120\\
y = 20$$

So the hands meet at $(10\frac{1}{11})$ minutes past 2 o'clock.

### Problem 19: Watch Accuracy

Let's consider a watch that gains 5 seconds in every 3 minutes. If its hands coincide every 64 minutes, we need to find how much it loses per day.

The time the watch gains per minute is:

$$\frac{5}{180} = \frac{1}{36}$$

Since there are 60 minutes in an hour and 24 hours in a day, the total gain per day is:

$$(\frac{1}{36}) \times (60 \times 24) = 20$$ seconds

However, we need to subtract this from 64 to find out how much it loses:

$64 - 20 = 44$$ minutes

The watch loses $44(8/11)$ minutes per day.

### Problem 22: Right Angle Between Hands

We want to find when the two hands of a clock are at a right angle (90°) between 4 and 5 o'clock. Let's consider the angles formed by these two hands:

*   Angle of H from the 12 o'clock position = $30x^\circ$, where x is the hours.
*   Angle of M from the 12 o'clock position = $6y^\circ$, where y is the minutes.

The sum of these angles should be equal to 90° or 270°:

$$30x + 6y = 90$$

We want to find when the hands are at a right angle between 4 and 5 o'clock. In this case, x = 4 (hour) and we need to solve for y (minutes).

Solving for y:

$$(30 \times 4) + 6y = 90\\
120 + 6y = 90\\
6y = -30\\
y = -5$$

However, since there are only 60 minutes in an hour, we need to add 60 to the result to get:

$$(30 \times 4) + 6(y+1) = 90\\
120 + 6y + 6 = 90\\
126 + 6y = 90\\
6y = -36\\
y = -6$$

However, we still need to add 60:

$$(30 \times 4) + 6(y+1+2) = 90\\
120 + 6y +12 = 90\\
132 + 6y = 90\\
6y = -42\\
y = -7$$

Adding 11 minutes to y gives us:

$$(30 \times 4) + 6(1+(-7)+11) = 90\\
120 + 6 = 90$$


So the hands are at a right angle for the first time at $(58/11)$ minutes past 4 o'clock.

# Mirror Images of Clock Times

## Introduction

The concept of mirror images in relation to clock times involves understanding how the positions of hour and minute hands are reflected when viewed from a mirror.

## Definition

A mirror image of a time on the clock is obtained by reversing the x-coordinate (hour) and y-coordinate (minutes) of the original time, effectively flipping it horizontally.

## Formulas

None applicable.

## Key Concepts

- The hour hand moves 360° in 12 hours, which means it moves 30° per hour.
- The minute hand moves 360° in 60 minutes, which means it moves 6° per minute.
- When viewed from a mirror, the reflection of the time on the clock remains unchanged.

## Key Takeaways

1. **Mirror Image Calculation**
To find the mirror image of a given time, follow these steps:
a. Identify the hour and minutes components of the original time.
b. Reverse the order of the hour and minute components (e.g., 3:45 becomes 5:43).
c. The resulting time is the mirror image.

2. **Direction of the Hour Hand**
